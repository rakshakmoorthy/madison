# CAJAL Figure Report — Competitor Signal Scan
_Density: recommend 3 figures, Mixed._

Three figurable concepts. First, the competitor matrix structure — the named primary output with a defined eleven-ish-column schema (competitor, source URL, capture date, headline/claim, proof, CTA, audience signal, tone label, channel, contradiction flag, human-review note) — is a structural schematic, though it must be split for the ≤6–8-component rule into a focused subset. Second, the agent-human division (agent: gather, extract, normalize, flag contradictions, identify patterns; human: define set, assess relevance, interpret meaning, decide implications) is a comparison/systems diagram and the chapter's clearest division-of-labor claim. Third, the observation-to-implication boundary — observations and sources on one side, the gate, then labeled implications on the other — is a process boundary that carries the "matrix is an observation layer, not a strategy layer" thesis. The contradiction-frequency bar chart in the manuscript is illustrative and thin (hypothetical counts); it is folded into the matrix figure as a column rather than given a standalone chart, to avoid implying false precision.
