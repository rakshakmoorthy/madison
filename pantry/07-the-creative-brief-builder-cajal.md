# CAJAL Figure Report — The Creative Brief Builder
_Density: recommend 3 figures, Mixed._

Three figurable concepts. First, the four field-status labels (Confirmed, Inferred, Unknown, Approval-needed) are a taxonomy applied to every brief field — the chapter's organizing device. Second, the brief field × evidence threshold mapping (objective, audience, insight, single-minded proposition, tone, mandatories — each with a different required status and evidence bar) is a structural schematic that carries the chapter's most specific teaching: the evidence threshold varies by field. Third, the brief → assumption register → gate → asset generation flow, where Inferred/Unknown fields must not be treated as approved strategy before someone explicitly resolves them or accepts the risk, is a process flowchart with a decisive gate. The "tension vs comfortable brief" idea is conceptually important but prose-bound (a quality of language, not a structure), so it is not figured.

## Figure 1 — The Four Field-Status Labels
Priority: Critical · Type: hierarchy/taxonomy

BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK:
Create a flat vector taxonomy for a single-column textbook page showing the four status labels that every brief field must carry. Arrange four equal boxes as a row or a 2x2 grid: Confirmed (the field reflects approved organizational inputs), Inferred (a reasonable interpretation of evidence, not yet reviewed), Unknown (the information needed is not currently available, and the field should say so), and Approval-needed (a strategic choice not yet made by someone with authority). Give each box a reserved header label slot and a reserved area beside it where a one-line definition would sit. Encode status with color: a confident supportive tone for Confirmed, a moderate tone for Inferred, a neutral cautionary tone for Unknown, and a distinct attention tone for Approval-needed. Keep the four boxes equal in size to signal these are categories of epistemic readiness, not a quality ranking. Thin strokes, flat fills, white background, generous spacing, and no baked-in words.

BLOCK 2 — FULL SCOPE:
[S - SPECIFICATION] Single-column 89mm, ≥300 DPI, vector, Okabe-Ito, white background.
[C - CONTENT] Four labels: Confirmed, Inferred, Unknown, Approval-needed, each with a reserved definition slot.
[O - ORGANIZATION] Four equal boxes (row or 2x2); reserved header and definition slots; no arrows.
[P - PRESENTATION] Flat vector, Okabe-Ito hex (Confirmed #009E73, Inferred #56B4E9, Unknown #E69F00, Approval-needed #CC79A7), 1pt strokes, white background, no baked text, no style suggestions.

## Figure 2 — Brief Fields and Their Evidence Thresholds
Priority: Critical · Type: structural schematic

BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK:
Create a flat vector structural schematic for a single-column textbook page mapping each creative-brief field to the evidence status it requires. Build a vertical list of six field rows: Objective, Audience, Insight, Single-Minded Proposition, Tone, and Mandatories. To the right of each field, place a reserved status marker slot and a reserved evidence-note slot. Use color on each status marker to show the field's required readiness: Objective should reach Confirmed; Audience should be Confirmed but is most often only Inferred; Insight is Inferred at best; the Single-Minded Proposition is Approval-needed until a claim-and-proof review occurs; Tone is an owned taste judgment; Mandatories must be Confirmed from a documented source. Visually distinguish the Proposition row as the highest-exposure field by giving its status marker the strongest attention accent, since a proposition that outruns its proof is a legal and reputational exposure. Keep field rows evenly spaced, status markers as small flat color chips, strokes thin, fills flat, background white, and no baked-in words.

BLOCK 2 — FULL SCOPE:
[S - SPECIFICATION] Single-column 89mm, ≥300 DPI, vector, Okabe-Ito, white background.
[C - CONTENT] Six field rows: Objective (Confirmed), Audience (Confirmed target / often Inferred), Insight (Inferred at best), Single-Minded Proposition (Approval-needed), Tone (owned taste judgment), Mandatories (Confirmed/documented). Proposition row emphasized as highest exposure.
[O - ORGANIZATION] Vertical field list; per-row reserved status-marker chip and evidence-note slot; Proposition row accented.
[P - PRESENTATION] Flat vector, Okabe-Ito hex (Confirmed chips #009E73, Inferred chips #56B4E9, Approval-needed chips #CC79A7, taste judgment chip #E69F00, Proposition accent #D55E00), 1pt strokes, white background, no baked text, no style suggestions.
[E - EXCLUSIONS] No rendered field text, no icons, no scoring, no arrows, no implication all fields share one threshold.

## Figure 3 — Brief, Register, and the Gate Before Asset Work
Priority: Important · Type: process flowchart

BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK:
Create a flat vector process flowchart for a single-column textbook page showing how a brief moves to asset generation through an explicit gate. Lay out the flow left to right: a Brief node (its fields each carrying a status), feeding into an Assumption Register node (which lists every Inferred and Unknown field with a resolution owner), then into a prominently emphasized Gate node, then into an Asset Generation node. At the Gate, show two single-headed outgoing paths that both cross forward: one path where the open fields have been resolved, and one path where a named person has explicitly accepted the risk of proceeding with known gaps. Reserve an annotation area at the Gate stating its condition: no downstream asset work treats an Inferred or Unknown field as approved strategy. Make clear that the failure mode is a third, forbidden path where the brief moves forward by default with invisible gaps — represent this as a blocked or severed path using the blocking color. Use a primary tone for Brief and Register, a dominant tone for the Gate, single-headed arrows, thin strokes, flat fills, white background, and no baked-in words.

BLOCK 2 — FULL SCOPE:
[S - SPECIFICATION] Single-column 89mm, ≥300 DPI, vector, Okabe-Ito, white background.
[C - CONTENT] Flow: Brief (status-labeled fields) → Assumption Register (Inferred/Unknown fields + owners) → Gate → Asset Generation. Two permitted crossing paths (gaps resolved; risk explicitly accepted by named person). One blocked forbidden path (proceed by default with invisible gaps). Gate condition annotation.
[O - ORGANIZATION] Left-to-right sequence with single-headed → arrows; Gate emphasized with two forward paths and one blocked path (⊣ blocking marker); reserved label and annotation slots.
[P - PRESENTATION] Flat vector, Okabe-Ito hex (Brief/Register #56B4E9, Gate #0072B2, permitted paths #009E73, blocked path #D55E00, arrows Black #000000), 1pt strokes, white background, no baked text, no style suggestions.
[E - EXCLUSIONS] No literal door/lock imagery, no rendered field text, no icons, no scoring, no person figures.
