# CAJAL Figure Report — Introduction (Madison Plus One)
_Density: recommend 1 figure, Foundational._

The Introduction is short and conceptual. It carries one load-bearing distinction worth a figure: the boundary between execution (producing an artifact) and judgment (deciding whether the artifact should exist, is right, fits, and who is accountable). This is the book's recurring concept and earns a single conceptual-map figure. The organizational list of chapters and the "how to read" guidance are navigational, not data-bearing, and need no figure.

## Figure 1 — The Execution–Judgment Boundary
Priority: Important · Type: conceptual map

BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK:
Create a flat vector conceptual map for a single-column textbook page that contrasts two domains of work separated by a vertical dividing boundary. On the left, place a region representing execution: the production of an artifact, the cheap and fast part of the work. On the right, place a region representing judgment: the disciplined decision about whether an artifact should exist, whether it is right, whether it fits the situation, what it omits, and what consequences follow. Draw a single bold vertical boundary line down the center, and a single horizontal arrow crossing from the left region into the right region to show that an artifact must pass from execution into judgment before it can be trusted. Keep the left region in a lighter primary tone and the right region in a stronger dominant tone to signal that judgment is where accountability concentrates. Use only flat shapes, simple rectangles for the two regions, and reserved areas where labels would later be placed. Do not bake any words into the image. Keep the composition balanced, calm, and uncluttered, with generous white space and no decorative elements.

BLOCK 2 — FULL SCOPE:
[S - SPECIFICATION] Single-column 89mm width, ≥300 DPI, vector, Okabe-Ito palette, white background.
[C - CONTENT] Two opposed regions: execution (artifact production) and judgment (decision about existence, correctness, fit, omission, consequence, accountability). One boundary between them. One crossing arrow indicating an artifact moving from execution toward judgment. [inferred] relative emphasis on judgment as the larger-stakes side.
[O - ORGANIZATION] Left region = execution; right region = judgment; central vertical boundary; single → arrow left-to-right crossing the boundary; reserved label slots within each region and beneath the arrow.
[P - PRESENTATION] Flat vector, Okabe-Ito hex (execution primary #56B4E9, judgment dominant #0072B2, boundary Black #000000), 1pt strokes, white background, no baked text, no style suggestions.
[E - EXCLUSIONS] No icons of people, no gauges, no checklists, no gradients, no third region, no numeric values, no chapter references, no decorative framing.

BLOCK 3 — NEGATIVE PROMPT:
people, gauges, icons of tools, numbers, percentages, extra regions, text labels, words, gibberish letters, titles, captions, decorative borders, realistic textures, drop shadows, gradient backgrounds, photographic elements, dual-headed arrows, hand-drawn styles, human figures, visual clutter, watermarks, red-green color combinations, rainbow color scales, 3D perspective distortion
