## Figure 2 — The Four Gate Decision Types
Priority: Critical · Type: comparison panels

**BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK**
Render a four-panel comparison of the gate decision types available at any point in a Madison run, arranged to show their distinct characters: proceed, conditional proceed, hold, and close with findings. For each panel, depict the decision as a small flow fragment with a forward arrow whose treatment encodes the decision's nature. Proceed: a clean unobstructed forward arrow into the next phase. Conditional proceed: a forward arrow that carries a small attached open-items marker forward, signaling movement with documented unresolved items. Hold: a forward arrow stopped at a barrier, with a marker naming the blocker and the person who can resolve it. Close with findings: a terminal node with a side branch depositing open questions into a future-work holding area. Color proceed in the active color, conditional proceed in the primary color, hold in the blocking color, and close with findings in the transitional color. The panel should communicate that none of these is a failure — a hold is the system working correctly and a conditional proceed is an accurate description, not a weak proceed. Keep to the four panels. No baked text; the differing arrow treatments and the per-type markers carry the meaning.

Confirmed: none is a failure.
