## Figure 1 — The Three Layers of a Performance Report
Priority: Critical · Type: hierarchy/taxonomy

**BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK**
Render a vertical three-tier stack representing the layers of a performance report, ordered from foundation to apex: description at the bottom, interpretation in the middle, recommendation at the top. Draw each tier as a horizontal band, stacked so the report visibly builds upward from facts to action. Annotate each tier with two qualities using small adjacent glyphs: what it contains and who is accountable. The description tier carries metric values, source files, and export dates, and is marked as the layer AI can extract and format. The interpretation tier carries baseline comparisons, anomaly flags, and signal-versus-noise judgment, marked as a layer AI can surface candidates for but where the human decides meaning. The recommendation tier carries action, next test, and decision owner, marked as a layer AI cannot supply and the human is accountable for. Use an ascending color treatment so accountability shifts visibly from machine-assisted at the base to human-owned at the top. Place a thin boundary between interpretation and recommendation marking the accountability shift. Keep to the three tiers. No baked text; the stack order and the per-tier accountability glyphs carry the meaning.
