# CAJAL Figure Report — Verifying Brand Evidence
_Density: recommend 4 figures, Mixed._

Four strong figure candidates. First, the verb-strength ladder (from "is associated with" up through "caused/proves") maps verbs to the evidence each requires — a hierarchy/ladder that is the chapter's central device. Second, the four-quadrant evidence-quality map (coverage × recency, with method/sample as a third dimension) is an explicit two-axis conceptual map. Third, the four-list sort (can say / can suggest / cannot claim / needs human review) is a taxonomy that channels every claim. Fourth, the evidence workflow with a human four-list-sort gate (Data → AI Analysis → Artifact → GATE → Rewrite → Recommendation) is a process flowchart with a critical highlighted gate. The before/after warranted-verb rewrite table is prose/template and is folded into the verb ladder figure.

## Figure 3 — The Four-List Sort
Priority: Important · Type: hierarchy/taxonomy

BLOCK 1 — ILLUSTRAE PASTE BLOCK:
Create a flat vector four-bucket taxonomy for a single-column textbook page showing how claims get sorted by what the evidence warrants. Arrange four equal containers, either as a 2x2 grid or four parallel columns: Can Say (conclusions the evidence directly and fully supports), Can Suggest (patterns worth flagging but not strong enough to recommend), Cannot Claim (conclusions the evidence does not support regardless of how confident they sound), and Needs Human Review (ambiguous cases requiring contextual judgment). Give each container a reserved header label slot and several empty reserved slots inside where individual claims would later be placed. Use color to encode warrant level: a confident supportive tone for Can Say, a moderate tone for Can Suggest, a cautionary tone for Cannot Claim, and a distinct transitional tone for Needs Human Review. Make the four containers equal in size to signal that the goal is not to move everything into Can Say but to know which bucket each claim belongs in.
