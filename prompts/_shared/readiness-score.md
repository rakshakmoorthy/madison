# readiness-score.md (shared)

A 0–100 readiness rubric, five dimensions × 20 points. (Superset of Nina `/ready` and the
Madison-Pitch "Shut Up And Take My Money" test.) Score from a skeptical reader's seat;
deduct for vagueness; end with the single highest-priority fix.

## The five dimensions (0–20 each)

1. **Clarity / Strategic clarity** — Is the core message (UVP / the ask / the claim)
   unambiguous and specific? Could someone read it and repeat exactly what this is?
   *Deduct for:* vague language, multiple competing messages, no clear audience.

2. **Distinctiveness** — Does it occupy territory a competitor could not claim?
   *Deduct for:* generic archetypes/positioning without specific execution; any claim
   that could appear on a competitor's site unchanged.

3. **Audience fit / Simplicity** — Does the language and framing feel made for the stated
   audience — not just inoffensive to everyone? Could a non-expert grasp it in 30 seconds?
   *Deduct for:* demographic generalizations, mismatched tone, no specific human truth.

4. **Internal consistency / Evidence** — Does every claim trace to something real (a
   feature, credential, behavior, run)? Does the message show up consistently across the
   pieces (voice ↔ visuals ↔ tagline ↔ proof)?
   *Deduct for:* claims with no evidence; a confident voice paired with a timid visual;
   numbers with no source.

5. **Execution readiness** — Could a designer/writer/developer/conductor pick this up and
   begin without a follow-up meeting?
   *Deduct for:* missing specifics (hex codes, dates, success conditions), vague
   directions that require interpretation.

## Output
- Score per dimension with 1–2 sentence justification; total /100.
- **One priority fix:** "Before this ships, change [X]."
- **Threshold:** 80+ = ready. Below 80 = name the two things to do next.
- **The cap (relative version):** if used to compare a cohort, depth is a floor under the
  bands — being best of a shallow pool does not earn top marks.
