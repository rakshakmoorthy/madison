# competitive-method.md (shared)

The observe → label → interpret discipline for any competitive/market analysis.
(BRANDY's spine; also Nina `/competitor` and Madison-Pitch Slide 7.) The point is
evidence-traced comparison, never vibes.

## Observation first, judgment second
Document what each competitor does before drawing any conclusion. The matrix is
evidence; the memo is argument. Do not collapse them.

## Label everything
- **[Observed]** — directly verifiable from public-facing content.
- **[Inferred]** — logical deduction from observable signals.
- **[Unverifiable]** — requires firsthand access; flag for manual check.
- **[Not Found]** — searched, no presence detected (note if absence looks strategic or accidental).

Never assert as **[Observed]** anything you did not see. Never claim a named person/org
"built nothing" / "has no X" unless verifiable — falsifiable overclaims cost you the room.

## No vanity metrics
High follower/star/volume counts without context are noise. Always ask: *by what measure,
compared to whom, and is it working?*

## Per competitor, capture
- What they own (visually and verbally) · their audience · their apparent archetype ·
  their single strongest asset (the thing you'd take if you could) · their most
  exploitable weakness. Each cell carries its evidence label.

## Then find the white space
What territory does no one own? Position relative to the map:
"[Brand] occupies the space between [A]'s [quality] and [B]'s [quality] — which no one
currently owns." Name real competitors; "industry players" is not useful. If you don't
know the specific competitors, ask before proceeding.

## A note is an interpretation with evidence
Not "posts regularly, good visuals" but "[platform] shows [evidence] suggesting
[strategic interpretation]; compared to [competitor], [ahead/behind/at parity] on
[dimension] because [signal]."
