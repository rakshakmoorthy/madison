# archetypes.md (shared)

The 12 Jungian brand archetypes, used as a **reflection/diagnosis instrument** — a
labeled self-test with contested validity, never a verdict. Cite it as what it is.
Shared by Nina (`/n2`), Madison Pitch (Slide 1), and the book's archetype chapter.

## The 12
Innocent · Everyman · Hero · Outlaw · Explorer · Creator · Ruler · Magician · Lover ·
Caregiver · Jester · Sage.

## How to apply (not generically — to THIS brand)
1. **Primary archetype.** Choose one. Explain why it fits *this* brand specifically
   (not the archetype in the abstract); name the **shadow risk** (what it becomes when it
   goes wrong); name one well-known brand that lives it well, and why it works.
2. **Secondary archetype** that adds nuance or productive tension. Explain how the two
   coexist without canceling each other out.
3. **Archetype brief**, exact format:
   "[Brand] is a [Primary] with a [Secondary] edge. It believes [core belief]. It speaks
   to people who [audience truth]. It will never [hard no]."
4. **Flag mismatches.** If what they *want* to be diverges from what the evidence
   (intake / resume / behavior) supports, name it directly.

## Discipline
The archetype is a prompt for thought, not a result. Keep the raw self-test output in
private reflection; let it *inform*, never *dictate*, the positioning. Visual/voice
choices derive from the archetype but must still be defended on their own evidence.
