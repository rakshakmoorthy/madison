# jargon-audit.md (shared)

Flag every term a non-specialist (client, investor, board, general reader) would not
immediately understand, and replace it. (Superset of the Nina and Madison-Pitch jargon
audits.)

## Rate every flagged term
- **RED** — never say this to an external audience; replace immediately.
- **YELLOW** — rephrase for context; acceptable internally, not in deliverables.
- **GREEN** — clear to a non-specialist as-is.

## Translation guide (extend with `destination-language.md`)
- "Brand archetype" → "brand personality" (GREEN after one definition)
- "SMP / Single-Minded Proposition" → "core message"
- "UVP" → "what makes you different"
- "Touchpoints" → "every place a customer encounters the brand"
- "Above/below the fold" → describe literally
- "Omnichannel" → DELETE — name the specific channels
- "Authentic / storytelling / innovative / seamless" → DELETE
- "Deliverables" → "what I'll hand you"
- "Ideation" → "developing ideas"
- Technical terms → plain business outcomes; process descriptions → customer-experience
  descriptions; "engine" language → "destination" language (see `destination-language.md`).
- Category-specific clichés: flag any phrase overused in this brand's industry that has
  lost meaning.

## Output
1. Flagged-terms table: Term | Rating | Replacement.
2. Full rewrite with all RED and YELLOW terms replaced.
3. One-sentence verdict on jargon density: "This reads as
   [professional / industry-insider / inaccessible] because …".
